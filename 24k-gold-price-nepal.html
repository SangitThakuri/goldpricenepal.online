What is 24K Fine Gold?

24K gold — also called Fine Gold or Asalchandie in Nepal — is the purest commercially available form of gold, with a purity of 99.9% (fineness 999). Unlike 22K or 18K gold, it contains virtually no other metals. This extreme purity gives 24K gold its characteristic deep yellow colour and makes it the most valuable form per gram.

In Nepal, 24K gold is primarily sold as coins, bars, and bullion for investment purposes. Most jewellery in Nepal is made in 22K (Tejabi) gold because 24K is too soft and malleable for everyday wear — it scratches and bends easily.

How FENEGOSIDA Sets the 24K Gold Price

Every trading morning, FENEGOSIDA (Federation of Nepal Gold and Silver Dealers' Association) announces the official buying and selling rate for 24K Fine Gold in Nepal. Their calculation process involves:

  • The international gold spot price from the London Bullion Market Association (LBMA) in USD per troy ounce
  • The current USD to NPR exchange rate set by Nepal Rastra Bank
  • Nepal's import duties: approximately 10% customs duty + 13% VAT
  • Standard dealer margins to cover logistics and handling

This is why Nepal's gold price is significantly higher than simply converting the international spot price. The taxes alone add roughly 23% to the base price.

24K Gold Price Per Gram in Nepal

The price per gram is derived by dividing the per-tola price by 11.664 (since 1 tola = 11.664 grams). This conversion is critical because international gold prices are always quoted in troy ounces (31.1035g) while Nepal uses tola and gram.

24K vs 22K vs 18K Gold — Which Should You Buy?

  • 24K (99.9%): Best for investment — coins, bars, and bullion. Highest purity, highest price, not suitable for jewellery.
  • 22K Tejabi (91.7%): Best for jewellery — durable enough for rings, bangles, necklaces. Most popular in Nepal.
  • 18K (75%): Mixed with other metals for very hard, durable jewellery. Often used for diamond-set pieces.
  • 14K (58.3%): Least pure gold jewellery. Very durable, much lower cost per gram.

If you are buying gold as a long-term investment, prefer 24K gold coins or bars. If buying jewellery, 22K Tejabi is the Nepal standard and offers the best balance of purity and workability.

Gold Measurement Units Used in Nepal

  • 1 Tola (तोला / Bhori) = 11.664 grams — the primary unit for gold pricing in Nepal
  • 1 Aana (आना) = 1/16 Tola = 0.7290 grams
  • 1 Lal (लाल) = 1/10 Aana = 0.0729 grams
  • 1 Gram = 0.08573 Tola
