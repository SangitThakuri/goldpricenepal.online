Gold Price in Kathmandu (काठमाडौं) — How It Works

As Nepal's capital and the largest city, Kathmandu has the highest concentration of FENEGOSIDA-registered gold dealers — particularly in the New Road, Indra Chowk, and Ason Chowk areas.

The gold price you see here is the official FENEGOSIDA rate, which applies uniformly across all of Nepal. There is no separate "Kathmandu gold price" — every FENEGOSIDA-registered dealer in Kathmandu uses the same base rate announced each morning.

Gold Markets in Kathmandu

The main gold trading areas in Kathmandu are: New Road (Juddha Sadak), Indra Chowk, Ason Chowk, Putalisadak.

New Road (Juddha Sadak) is Nepal's most famous jewellery market, with over 300 gold shops. Indra Chowk is the traditional gold wholesale area.

What Affects the Gold Price in Kathmandu?

  • International spot price — gold is a global commodity priced in USD
  • USD/NPR exchange rate — set by Nepal Rastra Bank daily
  • Import duties — Nepal charges ~10% customs duty on gold imports
  • Making charges — vary by shop and design (8–18% for jewellery); this is where Kathmandu shops may differ

Tips for Buying Gold in Kathmandu

  • Always check today's FENEGOSIDA rate on this page before visiting a shop
  • Verify the hallmark stamp: 999 (24K), 916 (22K), or 750 (18K)
  • Ask for a itemised bill showing gold weight, purity, rate, and making charges separately
  • Compare making charges across at least 2–3 shops before purchasing
  • Buy from FENEGOSIDA-registered dealers only
