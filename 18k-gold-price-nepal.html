What is 18K Gold (750 Fineness)?

18K gold contains 75% pure gold (750 fineness — meaning 750 parts per 1000 are gold) and 25% other metals such as copper, silver, nickel, or palladium. The alloy composition gives 18K gold several advantages over higher-purity gold for certain applications:

  • Hardness: Significantly harder than 22K or 24K, making it more scratch-resistant and durable
  • Setting suitability: Holds prong and bezel settings for diamonds and gemstones more securely
  • Design flexibility: Can achieve finer details and more intricate patterns
  • Price: Approximately 25% less expensive per tola than 24K gold

Where is 18K Gold Commonly Used in Nepal?

While 22K Tejabi remains the dominant gold for traditional jewellery, 18K is growing in popularity in Nepal, particularly for:

  • Diamond jewellery: Diamond-set rings, pendants, and earrings are almost always 18K globally — the harder alloy keeps settings secure
  • Lightweight modern designs: Young consumers prefer lighter, contemporary jewellery that 18K enables due to its strength-to-weight properties
  • Rose gold jewellery: Popular rose gold items use a copper-heavy 18K alloy (750 + higher copper ratio)
  • International brand jewellery: Most imported brand jewellery (Tanishq, Malabar, etc.) is 18K
  • Budget-conscious buyers: 18K costs ~25% less per tola than 24K while still containing substantial gold value

How to Calculate 18K Gold Value

The 18K price is always 75% (18/24) of the current 24K FENEGOSIDA rate:

  • Per tola: 24K rate × 0.75
  • Per gram: (24K per-tola rate ÷ 11.664) × 0.75
  • Example (Jul 2026): NPR 2,82,100 × 0.75 = NPR 2,11,575 per tola

Resale Value of 18K Gold

When selling 18K gold back to a dealer, the buyback calculation is:

Buyback value = weight × (18/24) × current 24K buying rate

Note that dealers may apply a small deduction for "melting loss" on resale, typically 1–2%. Always get quotes from multiple shops for significant amounts.

Hallmark Verification for 18K Gold

18K gold sold in Nepal should carry the 750 or 18K hallmark stamp on the piece. To verify:

  • Ask to see the stamp clearly — use a magnifying glass if needed
  • Request an acid test if you have any doubts about purity
  • Reputable shops will also offer XRF (X-ray fluorescence) testing for significant pieces
  • Always buy from FENEGOSIDA-registered dealers

18K Gold Price Nepal — FAQ

Common questions about 18K (750) gold in Nepal

Today's 18K gold price is shown live at the top of this page. It is calculated as 75% of the FENEGOSIDA 24K rate, updated daily.
18K gold is less commonly used for pure investment in Nepal compared to 24K. For investment purposes, 24K bars or coins are preferred since they contain the maximum gold per tola. However, 18K jewellery does retain significant gold value and can be sold back at a fair rate based on its gold content.
18K gold's higher alloy content makes it significantly harder than 24K or 22K gold. This hardness means prong settings, bezels, and channels that hold diamonds and gemstones are more secure and less likely to deform over time. That's why virtually all high-end diamond jewellery worldwide uses 18K or higher-karat alloys.
"750" is the fineness mark for 18K gold, indicating 750 parts per 1000 (75%) are pure gold. It is equivalent to 18 karat. When you see "750" or "18K" stamped on a piece of jewellery, it certifies that 75% of the metal is gold and 25% is other alloy metals.
