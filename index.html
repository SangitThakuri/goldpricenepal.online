About Nepal's Gold Market

Everything you need to know about buying and tracking gold in Nepal

📊 How Gold Prices Are Set in Nepal

Gold prices in Nepal are officially set each morning by FENEGOSIDA — the Federation of Nepal Gold and Silver Dealers' Association. They reference international spot prices (London Bullion Market Association), the current USD/NPR exchange rate, and applicable import duties.

Nepal levies a 10% customs duty and 13% VAT on gold imports, which makes local prices significantly higher than the raw international rate once converted.

  • Prices announced each morning before markets open
  • Separate rates for Tejabi (99.5%) and Asalchandie (99.9%) gold
  • Buying & selling rates differ by ~NPR 200–500/tola
  • Hallmarking mandatory for gold jewellery above 14K

⚖️ Gold Measurement Units in Nepal

Nepal uses several units to measure gold. Understanding these is essential when buying jewellery or comparing prices.

  • 1 Tola / Bhori = 11.664 grams (most common unit)
  • 1 Gram = 0.0857 tola (international standard)
  • 10 Grams = 0.857 tola (used in some quotes)
  • 1 Troy Ounce = 31.1035 grams = 2.667 tola
  • 1 Lal = 12 tola = 139.97 grams (rare)

The tola (also called bhori) is the dominant trading unit and all FENEGOSIDA official rates are quoted per tola.

🛒 Tips for Buying Gold in Nepal

  • Always buy from a registered FENEGOSIDA dealer
  • Check the hallmark stamp on jewellery (Bureau of Standards Nepal)
  • Compare today's FENEGOSIDA rate before purchasing
  • Negotiate making charges — typically 8–15% of gold value
  • Request a receipt showing gold weight, purity, and rate
  • Avoid street vendors or unregistered shops
  • 24K gold is for investment; 22K is better for jewellery

💡 Gold Purity Guide

Gold purity is measured in karats. Pure gold is 24 karats (24K = 99.9% gold). Higher karat gold is more valuable but softer and less durable.

  • 24K (99.9% pure) — Investment grade, very soft
  • 22K (91.67% pure) — Best for jewellery in Nepal
  • 18K (75% pure) — Durable, popular for fine jewellery
  • 14K (58.3% pure) — Most durable, used in fashion jewellery

In Nepal, most jewellery is sold in 22K or 24K gold. Always verify the purity stamp before purchasing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about gold prices and the Nepal gold market

1 tola equals 11.664 grams. The price of 1 tola of 24K gold in Nepal typically ranges from NPR 110,000 to 135,000 depending on international gold rates and the NPR/USD exchange rate. Check the live price at the top of this page.
Nepal imposes a 10% customs duty and 13% VAT (Value Added Tax) on imported gold. Additionally, dealers add their margin and transportation costs. These factors typically make Nepal gold prices 20–27% higher than the international spot price when converted to NPR at the market exchange rate.
FENEGOSIDA (Federation of Nepal Gold and Silver Dealers' Association) is the official body that sets daily gold prices in Nepal. Every morning, they announce the official buying and selling rates based on international spot prices from the London Bullion Market, current USD/NPR exchange rates, and government-mandated taxes.
22K gold is better for jewellery because it is harder and more durable than 24K. 24K gold (99.9% pure) is very soft and can scratch or bend easily, making it impractical for everyday jewellery. 22K gold (91.7% pure) maintains a beautiful golden color while being strong enough for rings, necklaces, and bangles.
Making charges are the fees jewellers charge for crafting gold into jewellery. In Nepal, making charges typically range from 8% to 15% of the gold value, depending on the complexity of the design.
