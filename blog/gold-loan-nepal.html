Gold Loan in Nepal: Banks, Interest Rates & How to Apply

Complete guide to gold loans in Nepal — which banks offer the best rates, current interest rates, LTV ratios, required documents, and repayment process.

Finance
📅 July 20, 2026 ⏱ 6 min read ✍️ goldpricenepal.online

Need quick cash but don't want to sell your gold? Nepal's banking system offers gold loans — a way to use your gold jewellery or bars as collateral to get instant funds, then reclaim your gold once you repay. Here's everything you need to know.

What Is a Gold Loan?

A gold loan (also called gold mortgage or gold-backed loan) is a secured loan where you pledge your gold to a bank or financial institution in exchange for a cash amount. The bank holds the gold safely until you repay the loan plus interest. You get your gold back upon full repayment.

Which Banks Offer Gold Loans in Nepal?

Most commercial banks and development banks in Nepal offer gold loans. Some prominent ones include:

  • Nepal Bank Limited (NBL)
  • Rastriya Banijya Bank (RBB)
  • Nabil Bank
  • Standard Chartered Bank Nepal
  • NIC Asia Bank
  • Himalayan Bank
  • Global IME Bank
  • Sanima Bank

Finance companies and microfinance institutions also offer gold loans, often with faster processing but sometimes higher interest rates.

Loan-to-Value (LTV) Ratio Explained

The LTV ratio determines how much you can borrow against your gold's value. Nepal Rastra Bank regulates LTV for gold loans.

💡 Example: You pledge 10 tola of 24K gold worth NPR 28,21,000 (at July 2026 prices). At 70% LTV, the maximum loan = NPR 19,74,700. The bank holds your gold until you repay this amount plus interest.

Documents Required

  • Citizenship certificate (original + copy)
  • Passport-sized photos (usually 2–3)
  • Gold purchase receipt or invoice (if available)
  • The gold itself — weighed and valued at the bank
  • Some banks may require PAN card

For amounts above NPR 10 lakh, banks may require additional KYC documents and source-of-funds declaration.

The Gold Loan Process Step by Step

  1. Approach the bank with your gold and ID documents
  2. Bank assays (tests) the gold purity — they typically use an acid test or XRF machine
  3. Gold is weighed and valued — bank uses the FENEGOSIDA rate for valuation
  4. Loan offer calculated — LTV % × gold value
  5. Agreement signed — you agree to repayment terms and interest rate
  6. Cash disbursed — often same day or within 24 hours
  7. Gold stored safely in bank vault until repayment

Repayment and What Happens If You Default

You can repay in instalments or as a lump sum within the agreed tenure (typically 6 months to 3 years). If you cannot repay and default on the loan, the bank has the legal right to auction your gold to recover the outstanding amount. Any surplus after recovering the loan is returned to you.
