Gold Coin Price in Nepal 2026 — 1 Tola, 5g & 10g Rates

Everything you need to know about buying gold coins in Nepal — current prices, where to buy, and how coin prices compare to jewellery rates.

Gold coins are one of the most popular ways to invest in gold in Nepal. They are easy to store, easy to resell, and their price is directly linked to the official FENEGOSIDA rate — unlike jewellery, which includes making charges. This guide covers gold coin prices in Nepal today, the available sizes, where to buy, and how coins compare to gold bars.

How Gold Coin Prices Are Calculated

The price of a gold coin in Nepal follows a simple formula:

Coin Price = (Weight in tola × FENEGOSIDA 24K Rate) + Minting Premium (0.5–2%)

For example, a 1 tola gold coin when the FENEGOSIDA rate is NPR 2,82,100:

  • Base price: NPR 2,82,100
  • 1% minting premium: NPR 2,821
  • Total: approximately NPR 2,84,921

This is significantly cheaper than 1 tola of gold jewellery, which would include 8–15% making charges on top of the gold price.

Where to Buy Gold Coins in Nepal

1. Licensed Gold Dealers (FENEGOSIDA Members)

The safest place to buy gold coins in Nepal is from licensed dealers registered under FENEGOSIDA. These dealers must follow the official daily rate and are regulated. Major markets include:

  • Indrachowk and New Road, Kathmandu — the main gold markets, hundreds of licensed dealers
  • Dharhara area, Kathmandu — wholesale gold market
  • Machhapokhari, Pokhara — main gold market in Pokhara
  • Juddha Road, Birgunj — active border-city gold market

2. Banks (Limited)

Some banks in Nepal (including Nepal Bank Limited and Rastriya Banijya Bank) sell gold coins occasionally, but availability is limited and premiums tend to be higher than dealer markets.

3. What to Avoid

  • Buying from unlicensed street vendors or online marketplaces without proper verification
  • Coins without a hallmark or assay certificate
  • Any seller quoting prices far below or above the FENEGOSIDA rate

For most Nepali investors, gold coins are the preferred choice because of their smaller denomination and high resale liquidity. A 1 tola coin can be sold at virtually any licensed gold dealer in Nepal within minutes.

Gold Coin vs Gold Jewellery in Nepal

If your goal is investment rather than wearing gold, coins are far more cost-effective than jewellery:

  • Jewellery includes 8–15% making charges which are lost at resale — dealers buy back at the raw gold rate only
  • Coins have only 0.5–2% minting premium, and that premium is partially recoverable when you resell
  • For a 1 tola purchase, you can save NPR 20,000–40,000 by buying a coin instead of jewellery

Tips for Buying Gold Coins in Nepal

  1. Always check today's FENEGOSIDA rate first — use our live price tracker before visiting a dealer
  2. Ask for a receipt — licensed dealers must provide one showing weight, purity, and price paid
  3. Check the hallmark — legitimate coins should have purity stamped (999 for 24K)
  4. Compare 2–3 dealers — premiums vary slightly between shops
  5. Buy during off-peak seasons — prices tend to be marginally lower outside Dashain/Tihar rush periods
  6. Keep your receipt safe — needed for resale verification

Is Buying Gold Coins a Good Investment in Nepal?

Gold has been Nepal's most reliable store of value for decades. Gold coin prices in Nepal have risen from around NPR 60,000 per tola in 2019 to over NPR 2,80,000 in 2026 — a gain of more than 360% in 7 years. However, Nepal's gold prices carry a structural premium of roughly 25–27% above the international spot price due to 10% customs duty and 13% VAT on imports.

This means that if international gold prices fall sharply, Nepal gold prices may not fall as much — but they also benefit less from any international price appreciation. Gold coins are a good hedge against rupee depreciation and inflation, but should be part of a diversified portfolio rather than your only investment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I buy gold coins online in Nepal?

There is no reliable regulated online marketplace for gold coins in Nepal as of 2026. Some dealers have websites, but physical pickup or verified delivery is still required. Avoid paying for gold coins on social media marketplaces without physically verifying the product.

What is the resale value of gold coins in Nepal?

Licensed dealers typically buy back gold coins at the FENEGOSIDA buying rate minus 0.5–1%. The minting premium you paid is largely recovered, making coins much better for resale than jewellery.

How do I verify if a gold coin is genuine?

Genuine 24K gold coins in Nepal should be stamped with "999" or "999.9" for purity. You can ask the dealer to weigh the coin in front of you and verify against the FENEGOSIDA rate. Licensed dealers also provide a receipt with weight and purity details.

What is the smallest gold coin I can buy in Nepal?

The most commonly available small coin is ½ tola (5.832 grams). Some dealers also sell 1 gram or 2 gram pieces, though these are less common and carry higher premiums.
