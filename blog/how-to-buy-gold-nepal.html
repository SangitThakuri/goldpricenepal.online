How to Buy Gold in Nepal: Complete 2025 Guide

Step-by-step guide to buying gold in Nepal safely — where to buy, what hallmarks to verify, fair pricing, avoiding scams, and best times to buy.

Guide
📅 July 20, 2026 ⏱ 7 min read ✍️ goldpricenepal.online

Buying gold in Nepal is a significant financial decision. Whether you're purchasing jewellery for a wedding, investing in gold bars, or buying as a gift, knowing the process protects you from overpaying or being misled. This complete guide walks you through every step.

Step 1: Know the Day's Official Rate

Before visiting any shop, check the current FENEGOSIDA rate on goldpricenepal.online. This is the official daily rate announced each morning. No legitimate dealer should charge significantly above this base rate (though jewellery will cost more due to making charges).

⚠️ Warning: Never buy gold without first checking the FENEGOSIDA rate. Some unscrupulous shops quote inflated "special rates" to unknowing buyers.

Step 2: Choose a Licensed Dealer

Only buy from FENEGOSIDA-registered gold dealers. Look for the federation's membership certificate displayed in the shop. In major cities:

  • Kathmandu: New Road, Indra Chowk, and Ason Chowk areas have the highest concentration of established gold shops
  • Pokhara: Lakeside and New Road areas
  • Birgunj, Butwal, Biratnagar: Main bazaar areas have reputable dealers

Established shops with long operating histories (10+ years) are generally safer. Ask neighbours or family for recommendations.

Step 4: Verify the Hallmark

Hallmarking confirms the gold's purity. In Nepal, look for:

  • 999 or 24K stamp — indicates 99.9% purity
  • 916 or 22K stamp — indicates 91.6% purity
  • 750 or 18K stamp — indicates 75% purity

The hallmark should be clearly stamped on the gold itself, not just the tag. If you're uncertain, ask the dealer to show you the stamp under a magnifying glass. You can also request a purity test (acid test) at the shop — reputable dealers will not object.

Step 5: Understand the Total Price

For jewellery, the final price includes:

  • Gold value: weight × FENEGOSIDA rate
  • Making charges (बनाउनी): 8–18% of gold value depending on design complexity
  • VAT: 13% on making charges only (not on gold itself)

Step 6: Weigh the Gold Yourself

Always ask to watch the gold being weighed on a calibrated scale. The displayed weight should match what's on the receipt. Bring a trusted person with you for large purchases.

Step 7: Demand a Proper Receipt

A legitimate receipt should include: shop name and licence number, date of purchase, gold type and purity, weight in tola or grams, rate per tola, making charges, and total amount paid. Keep this receipt — you'll need it if you want to sell the gold later.

Best Time to Buy Gold in Nepal

There's no perfect time, but some general guidance:

  • Avoid festival weeks (Dashain, Tihar): Demand surges, shop queues are long, and negotiating room shrinks
  • Watch for international dips: When global gold prices correct, Nepal's rate follows within 1–2 days
  • Weekday mornings: Shops are less crowded and staff have more time to assist you

Red Flags to Watch Out For

  • Price significantly above the FENEGOSIDA rate without explanation
  • No hallmark stamp on the jewellery
  • Refusal to weigh gold in front of you
  • Pressure tactics ("only available today", "last piece")
  • No proper receipt or a receipt without the shop's registration number
