Today's Gold Price in Nepal — What Drives It Daily

Understand how FENEGOSIDA sets Nepal's daily gold price, what factors move it every morning, and how to read the rate correctly.

Market
📅 July 20, 2026 ⏱ 5 min read ✍️ goldpricenepal.online

Every morning in Nepal, thousands of people check the gold price before making buying or selling decisions. But what actually determines the number you see? Understanding the mechanics behind Nepal's daily gold rate helps you make smarter decisions.

How Nepal's Daily Gold Price Is Set

FENEGOSIDA (Federation of Nepal Gold and Silver Dealers' Association) announces the official gold price each morning, typically between 10 AM and 11 AM Nepal Standard Time. This rate is followed by virtually every registered gold dealer across the country.

The calculation involves three main components:

  • International spot price — the live price of gold in USD per troy ounce from global commodity markets (COMEX/London Fix)
  • USD/NPR exchange rate — set by Nepal Rastra Bank (NRB); as of July 2026, approximately NPR 154–155 per USD
  • Import duties and taxes — Nepal charges a 10% customs duty plus other levies on gold imports, adding roughly 23–25% on top of the international price

💡 Quick formula: Nepal Gold Price ≈ (International spot price in USD/oz ÷ 32.15 troy oz per kg × 11.664g per tola ÷ 31.1g per troy oz) × USD/NPR rate × (1 + duties)

What Moves the Price Day to Day

1. International Gold Markets

Gold is priced globally. Events in the US, Europe, and the Middle East directly impact Nepal's rate. Key drivers include US Federal Reserve interest rate decisions, inflation data, geopolitical tensions, and USD strength. When the US dollar weakens, gold typically rises in USD terms — and therefore in NPR too.

2. USD/NPR Exchange Rate

Since gold is priced internationally in USD, a weaker Nepali Rupee means a higher gold price in NPR — even if international gold is flat. In recent years, the NPR has gradually weakened against the USD, contributing to rising domestic gold prices.

3. Local Demand and Supply

Nepal imports nearly all its gold. Festival seasons (Dashain, Tihar, wedding season from February to May) see spike in demand, which can push local premiums slightly higher than the formula-based price.

4. Government Policy

Import duty changes, gold quota limits, and NRB policies all affect the domestic price. When Nepal reduced gold import quotas in 2023 to protect foreign exchange reserves, local premiums widened significantly.

Why Does the Online Rate Sometimes Differ From the Jeweller's Price?

The FENEGOSIDA rate is the base rate for pure gold. When you buy jewellery, the final price includes:

  • Making charges (बनाउनी) — typically 8–15% of gold value
  • VAT on making charges (13%)
  • The dealer's own margin above the FENEGOSIDA rate

When to Track the Price

If you're planning to buy or sell gold, check the rate on multiple consecutive days to understand the recent trend. A single day's price can be volatile due to overnight international moves. For large purchases, consider tracking for at least a week and using the Historical Price Chart to spot patterns.
