Gold Purity Guide for Nepal: 18K, 22K & 24K Explained

What do gold purity numbers mean in Nepal? Complete guide to 18K, 22K, and 24K gold — differences in purity, price, best use, hallmark stamps, and verification.

Guide
📅 July 20, 2026 ⏱ 5 min read ✍️ goldpricenepal.online

Gold purity — measured in karats (K) or fineness numbers — tells you exactly how much of your gold is actually gold. Understanding purity is essential before any gold purchase in Nepal, whether you're buying jewellery or investment gold.

What Do the Numbers Mean?

Gold purity is expressed in two ways:

  • Karat (K): A scale of 24 parts. 24K = all 24 parts are gold = 100% pure. 22K = 22 of 24 parts are gold = 91.67% pure.
  • Fineness: Parts per 1000. 999 = 99.9% pure, 916 = 91.6% pure, 750 = 75% pure.

24K Gold (999 Fine Gold / Asalchandie)

24K gold is the purest form available commercially, containing 99.9% gold with only trace impurities. In Nepal, it is called Asalchandie (असल चाँदी) or simply "Fine Gold."

Properties:

  • Soft and malleable — easily bent or scratched
  • Deep, rich yellow colour
  • Does not tarnish or corrode
  • Highest resale value per gram

Used for: Investment bars, commemorative coins, gold biscuits, ceremonial items, and some traditional ornaments that aren't worn regularly.

22K Gold (916 Tejabi)

The most popular gold in Nepal's jewellery market. Called Tejabi (तेजाबी), it contains 91.67% gold alloyed with 8.33% other metals (typically copper and silver), which adds strength and durability while maintaining the classic gold appearance.

Properties:

  • Significantly harder than 24K — resists daily wear
  • Slightly paler yellow than 24K (barely noticeable)
  • Can be worked into intricate designs
  • Maintains value well at resale

Used for: Bangles, necklaces, earrings, rings, wedding jewellery, and all traditional Nepali gold ornaments.

💡 Resale calculation: When selling 22K gold, dealers calculate: weight × (22/24) × current 24K rate. So 1 tola of 22K = 0.9167 tola worth of 24K gold value.

18K Gold (750 Fineness)

18K gold contains 75% gold and 25% other metals. It's less traditional in Nepal but increasingly popular for:

  • Diamond and gemstone studded jewellery (the harder alloy holds settings better)
  • Rose gold jewellery (copper-heavy alloy gives pink tint)
  • Modern, lightweight designs
  • International brand jewellery

18K is approximately 25% less expensive per tola than 24K, making it attractive for jewellery buyers who want more design for their budget.

How to Verify Gold Purity

1. Hallmark Stamp

Look for the purity stamp directly on the gold piece. Common stamps in Nepal:

  • 999 or 24K → Fine Gold
  • 916 or 22K → Tejabi
  • 750 or 18K → 18 Karat

2. Acid Test

A reliable dealer will use an acid test (रासायनिक परीक्षण). Different acid concentrations react differently to different gold purities. A genuine 22K piece will show a specific reaction colour that differs from 18K or 24K.

3. XRF Machine

Premium jewellers and banks use X-ray fluorescence (XRF) testing machines that give an accurate purity reading in seconds without damaging the gold. If you're buying a significant quantity, ask for XRF verification.

What Colour Is "Real" Gold?

Many people mistakenly assume very bright/shiny yellow means higher purity. In reality:

  • 24K gold is a rich, warm, slightly orange-tinted yellow
  • 22K gold is similar but very slightly paler — nearly indistinguishable to the naked eye
  • Very bright, almost silvery-yellow items may indicate lower purity or plating
