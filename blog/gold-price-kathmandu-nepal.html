Kathmandu's Gold Markets

1. New Road (Juddha Sadak) — The Main Hub

New Road is Kathmandu's premier gold shopping street, with over 300 gold shops concentrated in 500 metres. It offers the widest selection of designs, from traditional Nepali jewellery to modern styles. Competition is high, which helps keep making charges reasonable.

  • Best for: Large purchases, comparing designs, wedding sets
  • Making charges: 8–14% for standard designs
  • Tip: Visit at least 3 shops on New Road before buying — prices and making charges vary significantly

2. Indra Chowk — The Wholesale Area

Indra Chowk, adjacent to the historic Akash Bhairab temple, is Kathmandu's traditional wholesale gold area. Many retail jewellers source from here. If you know someone in the trade, buying closer to wholesale here can save money.

  • Best for: Plain gold bars, wholesale jewellery, experienced buyers
  • Note: Some shops here primarily deal with other traders; individual buyers should bring knowledge of the FENEGOSIDA rate

3. Ason Chowk — Traditional Neighbourhood Market

Ason is Kathmandu's oldest trading area. Gold shops here serve the local Newar community and surrounding neighbourhood. Making charges can be higher for intricate traditional Newar designs (dhungri, tilhari), but the craftsmanship is often superior.

Kathmandu Gold Buying Tips

  • Always check the FENEGOSIDA rate before you go
  • Shop on weekday mornings — less crowded, more time to negotiate
  • For wedding gold, start shopping 2–3 months early to get designs made to order
  • Ask for the making charges to be shown as a percentage, not just a rupee amount
  • Verify all FENEGOSIDA-registered shops on your receipt
