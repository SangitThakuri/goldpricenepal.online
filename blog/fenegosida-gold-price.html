How FENEGOSIDA Sets Gold Prices in Nepal Every Day

Who is FENEGOSIDA, how do they calculate Nepal's daily gold price, when is the rate announced, and why does it sometimes differ from international spot rates?

Education
📅 July 20, 2026 ⏱ 5 min read ✍️ goldpricenepal.online

Every gold dealer in Nepal, from the smallest shop in Pokhara to the largest jeweller in Kathmandu's New Road, follows a single price source: FENEGOSIDA. Understanding this organisation is key to understanding how Nepal's gold market works.

What Is FENEGOSIDA?

FENEGOSIDA stands for the Federation of Nepal Gold and Silver Dealers' Association (नेपाल सुनचाँदी व्यवसायी संघ). It is the apex body of gold and silver traders in Nepal, established in 1975, with its headquarters in Kathmandu.

FENEGOSIDA has thousands of member dealers across Nepal's 77 districts. Membership is required to legally trade gold at the retail level. The federation:

  • Sets the official daily gold and silver price in NPR
  • Coordinates with Nepal Rastra Bank (NRB) on import quotas and policies
  • Advocates for the gold trade industry in government policy discussions
  • Runs training and certification for hallmarking

How They Calculate the Daily Price

Each morning, FENEGOSIDA officials meet and calculate the day's official rate using this process:

1. International Spot Price

They check the live international gold price from COMEX (New York commodity exchange) and/or the London Bullion Market Association (LBMA) Fix. This price is in USD per troy ounce.

2. Unit Conversion

The international price is converted from troy ounces to tolas. 1 troy ounce = 31.1035 grams. 1 tola = 11.664 grams. Therefore: 1 troy ounce = 2.6667 tolas.

3. Currency Conversion

The USD price is converted to NPR using the Nepal Rastra Bank's (NRB) official exchange rate for that day. This rate is published by NRB each morning and serves as the reference for all government and regulated transactions.

4. Import Duties and Levies

Nepal imposes the following on gold imports:

LevyRate
Customs duty10%
Agriculture development fee0.5%
Other applicable taxes~2–3%
Total premium over international~23–26%

5. Final Announcement

After applying all the above, FENEGOSIDA announces the official selling rate (what shops charge customers) and the buying rate (what shops pay when buying back gold from customers). The difference between these rates — typically NPR 1,000–2,500 per tola — is the dealer's margin, not FENEGOSIDA's.

⏰ Timing: The daily rate is usually announced between 10:00 AM and 11:00 AM NST (Nepal Standard Time). If the announcement is delayed or if international markets moved sharply overnight, the rate may be announced later or revised mid-day in rare cases.

Why Nepal's Price Differs from International Spot

You may notice that the gold price on international sites (in USD converted to NPR) is significantly lower than FENEGOSIDA's rate. This is entirely due to the import duties described above. Nepal's domestic price includes 23–26% more than the international equivalent, covering customs, levies, and dealer margins throughout the supply chain.

Can the Rate Change Mid-Day?

Rarely, but it does happen. If international gold makes an extreme move (e.g., +3% or more in a single session), FENEGOSIDA may issue a revised rate. This typically happens during major geopolitical events or significant US economic data releases. On most days, the morning announcement holds for the full trading day.

What Happens on Holidays?

If Nepal has a public holiday (e.g., national holidays, Dashain, Tihar), FENEGOSIDA does not announce a rate. Gold shops are typically closed. The previous business day's rate is treated as the reference if any emergency transactions occur.
