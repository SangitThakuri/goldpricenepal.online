24K vs 22K Gold in Nepal: Which Should You Buy?

Detailed comparison of 24K Fine Gold and 22K Tejabi gold in Nepal — purity, price difference, durability, and which is right for jewellery or investment.

Education
📅 July 20, 2026 ⏱ 6 min read ✍️ goldpricenepal.online

Walk into any gold shop in Nepal and you'll be asked immediately: "24K chahiyo ki 22K?" (Do you want 24K or 22K?). Understanding the difference helps you choose the right gold for your purpose — and avoid paying the wrong price.

Why 24K Is Too Soft for Jewellery

Pure 24K gold is beautiful but impractical for everyday jewellery. It scratches easily, bends under light pressure, and loses its shape over time. A 24K ring worn daily would show visible wear within months. That's why 8.33% copper and silver are mixed into 22K gold — these metals dramatically increase hardness without significantly affecting the gold's appearance or value.

When to Buy 24K Gold

  • Investment: Gold bars, coins, and biscuits are almost always 24K. When you want the highest gold content per rupee spent, choose 24K.
  • Gold coins as gifts: Ceremonial and religious gold coins (used in pooja, weddings) are typically 24K.
  • Storing value: If you're buying gold purely to hold value and don't plan to wear it, 24K gives you more gold per tola spent.

When to Buy 22K Gold

  • Jewellery: Rings, bangles, necklaces, earrings, and bracelets — virtually all traditional Nepali gold jewellery is 22K.
  • Wedding gold: Wedding sets (guniyo, cholo accessories, tilhari, pote, bangles) are traditionally 22K Tejabi.
  • Everyday wear: 22K is durable enough for daily use without excessive scratching.

💡 Nepali tradition: In Nepal, gold gifted at weddings (both sides) is almost universally 22K Tejabi. Very few families use 24K for wearable jewellery.

The Price Maths

As of July 2026, 22K is approximately NPR 23,508 cheaper per tola than 24K. For a 10-tola wedding set, that's over NPR 2.35 lakh in savings. However, when reselling 22K, you also get proportionally less (roughly 91.7% of the 24K resale value).

The key insight: the cost per gram of actual gold is nearly the same in both. You pay for the gold content, not the total weight. A dealer buying back 22K will calculate: weight × (22/24) × current 24K rate.

What About 18K Gold?

18K gold (75% pure) is less common in Nepal's traditional market but is growing in popularity for modern jewellery designs. It's harder than 22K, allows for more intricate settings, and costs significantly less per tola. It's ideal for studded jewellery with diamonds or gemstones.
