Best Time to Buy Gold in Nepal — When Is Gold Cheapest?

A practical guide to seasonal gold price patterns in Nepal — when to buy, when to wait, and why trying to perfectly time the market rarely works.

Buying Guide
📅 July 20, 2026✍️ goldpricenepal.online⏱ 6 min read

One of the most common questions from gold buyers in Nepal is: "Should I buy gold now or wait for the price to fall?" The honest answer is that no one can predict gold prices precisely — but there are genuine seasonal patterns in Nepal's gold market that can help you make a smarter decision about when to buy.

Nepal's Gold Price Seasonal Calendar

Nepal's gold market has distinct high-demand and low-demand seasons driven by festivals, weddings, and agricultural harvest cycles. Higher demand generally means higher prices — or at minimum, dealers with less incentive to negotiate.

When Is Gold Cheapest in Nepal? (Historical Pattern)

Based on historical FENEGOSIDA price data, Nepal gold prices tend to be relatively lower in January–March and June–July compared to the October–November festival peak. The difference is typically 2–6% between the seasonal low and the Dashain peak — not huge, but on a purchase of NPR 2,82,000 (1 tola), that's NPR 5,640–16,920 in savings.

Important: These are demand-side patterns within Nepal. International gold prices (driven by global markets) have a much bigger impact than local seasonal demand. A 10% international gold rally in October will push Nepal prices up regardless of local demand patterns.

The Real Answer: International Markets Matter More Than Seasons

Nepal gold prices are ultimately set by the international gold spot price plus a fixed premium for import duties and taxes. This means:

  • If international gold falls 10%, Nepal's gold price falls ~10% regardless of festival season
  • If international gold rises 5% during the "cheap" month of January, Nepal prices rise 5%
  • Local demand affects prices at the margin — maybe 1–3% — but international prices dominate

Someone who waited for January (historically cheap) in 2024 would have paid more than someone who bought in October 2023, because international gold rallied strongly in early 2024. The "seasonal timing" strategy does not always work.

5 Practical Tips for Buying Gold at the Right Time

  1. Buy when you need it, not when you think prices will be lower
    If you have a wedding in November, buying in August reduces the risk of prices rising. Waiting to time the exact bottom usually results in missing opportunities or panic-buying at the last minute.
  2. Use a regular buying strategy (SIP approach)
    Instead of buying all at once, spread your purchases over 3–6 months. This averages out the price — you avoid both the top and the bottom, which is actually better than most timed purchases.
  3. Avoid buying in the two weeks before Dashain
    This is when Nepal gold demand surges the most. Dealers have full order books and no incentive to discount. Making charges may also be non-negotiable during this period.
  4. Watch the USD/NPR exchange rate
    A strengthening dollar makes gold more expensive in NPR even if the international gold price in USD is flat. If the rupee weakens sharply, buy sooner rather than later.

Should You Buy Gold in Nepal Right Now (July 2026)?

Gold prices in Nepal as of late July 2026 are around NPR 2,82,100 per tola for 24K — approximately 4% below the April 2026 peak of NPR 2,93,000. The Dashain-Tihar festival season in October typically pushes prices up, which means buying now (before the festival demand surge) has historically been a reasonable strategy.

However, no one can guarantee that prices will not fall further before October. If prices do fall, you will have paid more than necessary. If they rise (as they did in most years 2020–2026), you will have bought at a good point.

Bottom line: If you have a genuine need (wedding, ceremony, investment plan), buy at today's rate. If you are speculating purely on price movements, that is a risk that no article can advise you on.

Best Time of Day to Buy Gold in Nepal

FENEGOSIDA announces the official daily gold rate each morning. The day's rate is typically fixed and all licensed dealers must follow it for that day. So there is no "better time of day" — a 9 AM purchase and a 4 PM purchase at the same dealer will be at the same FENEGOSIDA rate.

However, visiting dealers early in the morning (before 11 AM) means you have the whole day's inventory to choose from and dealers are less rushed. For large purchases, midweek visits (Tuesday–Thursday) when shops are less busy give you more time to negotiate making charges.

What About Buying Gold Online in Nepal?

As of 2026, there is no regulated online gold marketplace in Nepal. Physical gold must be purchased from licensed FENEGOSIDA dealers in person. Be extremely cautious of social media sellers or unverified websites claiming to sell gold — many are fraudulent. Always verify dealer licensing before any transaction.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it better to buy gold before or after Dashain?

Historically, buying 1–3 months before Dashain (August–September) tends to be cheaper than buying in the immediate pre-Dashain rush (last two weeks of September–early October). After Dashain, prices often soften for 4–8 weeks as demand cools.

Does gold price increase during Teej in Nepal?

Teej (August–September) drives significant demand for gold bangles and jewellery. Prices may firm slightly in the weeks before Teej as retailer buying increases, but the effect is smaller than Dashain–Tihar.

What month has the lowest gold price in Nepal?

Historically, January and February show relatively softer gold prices in Nepal due to low post-festival demand. However, this is not guaranteed — international markets can push prices in either direction at any time.

Is now a good time to invest in gold in Nepal?

Gold has risen over 300% in Nepal over the past 7 years. Whether it continues depends on international markets, USD/NPR movements, and global economic conditions.
