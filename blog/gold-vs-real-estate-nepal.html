Gold vs Real Estate in Nepal: Which Investment Wins in 2025?

A data-driven comparison of gold and real estate investments in Nepal — returns, liquidity, risk, taxes, and which one suits different investor profiles.

Investment
📅 July 20, 2026 ⏱ 8 min read ✍️ goldpricenepal.online

In Nepal, two investments dominate family financial planning across generations: gold and real estate (land/property). Both are deeply embedded in Nepali culture. But which one actually delivers better returns? And for whom? Here's a data-driven comparison.

Returns: Gold Has Won Recently

Over 2024–2026, gold in Nepal delivered approximately 29% annualised returns, significantly outperforming most real estate markets. Urban land in prime Kathmandu areas appreciated around 12–20% annually, while suburban and rural land appreciated less.

However, this comparison is misleading in isolation:

  • Real estate generates rental income (3–5% annual yield on property value), while gold generates no passive income
  • Gold's recent performance was exceptional — driven by unusual global factors that may not repeat
  • Leverage: Real estate can be purchased with a bank loan (30–40% down payment), amplifying returns. Gold loans exist but are typically shorter-term

📊 Real example: NPR 50 lakh invested in gold (2024) → worth ~NPR 1 crore (2026). Same amount in Kathmandu land in a good location → worth NPR 65–75 lakh plus ~NPR 2.5L in rental income. Gold won this round — but past performance is not a guarantee.

The Liquidity Advantage of Gold

This is gold's most underappreciated advantage in Nepal. You can walk into any gold shop and sell your gold within 30 minutes. For real estate, even a willing buyer needs 2–6 months for due diligence, bank loans, and registration.

In an emergency — medical, business, or otherwise — gold provides immediate cash. Real estate does not. Many Nepali families keep gold precisely for this reason: liquid wealth that can be deployed instantly.

The Real Estate Advantage: Leverage and Income

Real estate allows you to control a large asset with relatively little upfront capital. A NPR 50 lakh property bought with NPR 15 lakh down payment and a bank loan: if the property rises to NPR 65 lakh, your NPR 15 lakh earned NPR 15 lakh return — that's 100% on your equity, not the 30% the headline figure suggests.

This leverage effect means real estate can outperform gold on a per-rupee-invested basis in rising markets. Gold cannot be purchased on leverage in a similar way.

Tax Considerations

Gold: Nepal currently does not impose capital gains tax on profits from selling physical gold. This is a significant advantage that may change in the future as tax policy evolves.

Real estate: Capital gains tax applies on property sale profits. Rental income is also taxable. Transaction costs (registration fees, etc.) are 4–5% of property value, paid upfront.

The Ideal Approach: Both

Most Nepali families who have built substantial wealth hold both. A typical allocation might be:

  • 60–70% in real estate (primary residence + 1–2 investment properties)
  • 15–25% in gold (liquid wealth, emergency fund, cultural necessity)
  • 10–15% in financial instruments (FD, stocks, insurance)
