Nepal Gold Price History 2024–2026: Trends & Key Events

How gold prices moved in Nepal from 2024 to 2026 — key highs, lows, what drove each major price movement, and what it means for buyers and investors.

Analysis
📅 July 20, 2026 ⏱ 8 min read ✍️ goldpricenepal.online

Nepal's gold price has been on a remarkable upward trajectory over the past two years, reaching historic highs driven by global economic uncertainty, a weakening Rupee, and surging international demand. Here's a comprehensive look at how prices moved and what caused each major shift.

Mid-2024: The Bull Run Begins

In June 2024, Nepal's 24K gold price stood at approximately NPR 1,75,000–1,85,000 per tola. International gold was trading at around USD 2,300 per troy ounce following months of central bank buying, particularly from China, India, and emerging market economies diversifying away from the US dollar.

Key drivers in this period:

  • Global central banks buying gold at record pace (over 1,000 tonnes in 2023)
  • US Federal Reserve signalling possible rate cuts, weakening the dollar
  • Geopolitical tensions in Middle East increasing safe-haven demand

Late 2024: Record Breaking Prices

October–November 2024 saw Nepal's gold prices breach NPR 2,20,000 per tola for the first time. International gold crossed USD 2,700/oz on some trading sessions. Domestic factors compounded the increase:

  • Dashain and Tihar festival demand (October–November) pushed local premiums higher
  • USD/NPR rate climbed to NPR 134–136, adding to the domestic price
  • Limited import quotas kept supply tight relative to festival demand

📊 Key data point: Nepal's 24K gold price rose approximately 30% from June 2024 to December 2024 — outpacing most other asset classes in Nepal for the year.

Early 2025: Consolidation and Wedding Season

Prices stabilised in January–February 2025 around NPR 2,30,000–2,45,000 per tola. This is typical — post-festival consolidation as demand eases. However, the February–May wedding season brought prices back up. Cultural demand for gold during auspicious wedding dates kept purchases elevated.

Mid-2025: Global Fed Pivot

When the US Federal Reserve finally cut interest rates in early 2025, it weakened the US dollar significantly. A weaker dollar is historically bullish for gold, and Nepal felt the effect immediately. International prices climbed toward USD 2,900–3,000/oz, pushing Nepal's rate past NPR 2,60,000.

2026: The New Normal Above NPR 2.5 Lakh

By early 2026, NPR 2.5 lakh per tola had become the new floor for 24K gold. Prices have mostly traded between NPR 2,65,000 and 2,90,000. The combination of structural factors — continued central bank buying globally, USD weakness, and Nepal's own import duty structure — has kept prices elevated.

What This Means for Buyers

Looking at the 2-year trajectory, gold in Nepal has delivered approximately +29% annual returns (based on the historical data available on this site). This doesn't mean prices will continue rising at this rate — corrections are always possible — but it illustrates gold's role as a wealth preserver against inflation and currency depreciation.

What to Watch in Late 2026

  • US election cycle and Fed policy — interest rate direction remains the biggest global driver
  • Nepal's import policy — any change in gold import quotas or duties will directly impact local price
  • USD/NPR rate — further Rupee depreciation adds to domestic gold cost
  • Festival calendar — Dashain (October) typically brings seasonal demand spike
