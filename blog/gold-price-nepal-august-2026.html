Why Is August Important for Nepal Gold Buyers?

Shrawan Mondays (Shrawan Sombar)

The Shrawan month is considered highly auspicious for purchasing gold and jewellery in Nepal, particularly on Mondays (Shrawan Sombar). During this period, demand from religious and ceremonial buyers increases. The four Shrawan Mondays in 2026 fall on July 21, July 28, August 4, and August 11 — all popular dates for gold purchases.

Pre-Wedding Season Buying

August and September see families begin purchasing gold for autumn weddings. The wedding season in Nepal runs from October through December and February through April, and advance buying begins 2–3 months ahead to avoid festival-period price spikes.

Teej Festival (September)

Haritalika Teej falls in September 2026 — one of the most important festivals for women's gold jewellery purchases. August buying often accelerates in anticipation of Teej demand.

What's Driving Gold Prices in August 2026?

International Gold Spot Price

Nepal's gold price moves with international gold spot markets. As of late July 2026, international gold is trading around USD 3,200–3,350 per troy ounce — near all-time highs. Key international factors include:

  • US Federal Reserve interest rate policy — lower rates typically push gold prices higher
  • Geopolitical uncertainty globally — gold is a safe-haven asset
  • Central bank gold buying — several central banks have been increasing gold reserves
  • Inflation expectations — gold is seen as an inflation hedge

USD/NPR Exchange Rate

Nepal imports most of its gold and prices it in USD first. A weaker Nepali Rupee against the dollar means higher gold prices in NPR even if international gold is flat. The USD/NPR rate has been around 132–136 in 2026.

Import Duty and Taxes

Nepal levies 10% customs duty + 13% VAT on gold imports, adding approximately 25–27% to the international price. This structural premium means Nepal gold prices are always significantly above international parity.

Should You Buy Gold in August 2026?

Timing the gold market perfectly is impossible — even professional traders cannot consistently predict short-term price movements. However, some context for August buyers:

  • The recent pullback from the April–May 2026 peak (near NPR 2,93,000) has brought prices down about 4% — a modest but real discount
  • Dashain and Tihar demand (October 2026) historically lifts gold prices as demand surges — buying in August may be cheaper than waiting until October
  • If your need is immediate (wedding, ceremony), buy at today's rate — trying to time the market often means paying more later
  • If purely for investment, systematic monthly buying (similar to SIP in mutual funds) outperforms trying to time the market

Gold Price Outlook for Rest of 2026

Nepal gold prices typically follow a seasonal pattern:

  • August–September: Moderate demand, prices may consolidate
  • October (Dashain/Tihar): Peak demand, prices often 2–5% higher than pre-festival levels
  • November–December: Wedding season, sustained demand
  • January–February 2027: Slower season, potential for price softness

The longer-term outlook depends largely on international gold markets and USD/NPR movements. Most analysts expect gold to remain supported above USD 3,000/oz for the rest of 2026, which would keep Nepal's 24K rate above NPR 2,50,000 per tola.
