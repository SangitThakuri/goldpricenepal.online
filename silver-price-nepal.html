Silver (Chandi) Price in Nepal — Overview

Silver, known as Chandi (चाँदी) in Nepali, has been used for centuries in Nepal for religious items, silverware, ornaments, and utensils. FENEGOSIDA announces the official silver rate per tola each morning alongside gold prices, making it easy for buyers and sellers to reference a transparent market price.

Nepal's silver price is derived from the international silver spot price (in USD per troy ounce), converted to NPR, and adjusted for import duties and VAT. Silver carries the same 10% customs duty and 13% VAT as gold in Nepal.

How Silver Price is Calculated in Nepal

  • International spot price: Silver is priced in USD per troy ounce on global markets (LBMA, COMEX)
  • USD/NPR conversion: Applied at the Nepal Rastra Bank exchange rate
  • Import duties: ~10% customs duty + 13% VAT added on import
  • Tola conversion: 1 troy ounce = 2.6667 tola; price is converted accordingly

Silver vs Gold — Gold-Silver Ratio in Nepal

The gold-silver ratio measures how many ounces of silver are needed to buy one ounce of gold. Globally this ratio ranges from 60:1 to 90:1. In Nepal, silver is typically priced at around 1/65th to 1/80th the price of gold per tola.

This ratio matters for investors: a high ratio (e.g., 80:1) suggests silver is relatively cheap vs gold and may offer better upside potential. A low ratio (e.g., 50:1) suggests silver is relatively expensive.

Uses of Silver in Nepal

  • Religious items: Silver idols, plates (thalis), bowls, and ritual objects
  • Silverware: Cutlery, cups, and decorative pieces for gifting
  • Jewellery: Silver rings, bangles, anklets, especially in traditional and ethnic designs
  • Investment: Silver bars and coins as an affordable precious metal investment
  • Industrial: Electronics, solar panels — growing global industrial demand
