Gold vs Fixed Deposit in Nepal: What the Data Shows

Over the past 6 years (2020–2026), Nepal's 24K gold price rose from approximately NPR 85,000 per tola to NPR 2,82,100 — an annualised return of roughly 22% per year. Bank FDs in Nepal have offered 7–9% annually during the same period.

However, this comparison needs important caveats:

  • Gold returns are not guaranteed — the 2020–2026 period was exceptionally bullish due to COVID uncertainty, global inflation, and geopolitical events. Long-term (20-year) historical gold returns globally are closer to 8–12%/year.
  • FDs have no import duty handicap — Nepal's gold carries a ~23–25% import premium baked into the purchase price. You pay this upfront and only recover it as gold prices rise.
  • FDs earn income immediately — interest starts accruing day one. Gold only pays when you sell at a higher price.
  • FDs are fully liquid and insured — Nepal's Deposit and Credit Guarantee Fund (DCGF) insures up to NPR 5 lakh per bank. Gold has no such guarantee and requires secure storage.

Our Recommendation

A balanced approach works best for most Nepali investors:

  • FD for your emergency fund and short-term goals (under 3 years) — predictable returns, insured
  • Gold for long-term wealth preservation (5+ years), cultural requirements, and currency hedge
  • Target: 15–25% of savings in physical gold; remainder in FDs, stocks, and real estate
